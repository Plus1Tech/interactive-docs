Quick Wins — 4 Options

Effort: Low · Timeline: Days, not weeks · No new tools required

1

Optimized ChatGPT Prompt Library

Low Effort High Impact No Setup

Replace ad-hoc prompting with a copy-paste prompt kit for Yulia and the India social team. Covers every recurring content task in one document. Consistent inputs = consistent outputs = less editing time.

ReplacesFreeform ChatGPT sessions with no structure
ToolsClaude or ChatGPT
Used byYulia + India social manager
  • SEMrush brief → long-form SEO article (with keyword density, heading structure, length)
  • Article → email nurture summary
  • Article → 3 platform-specific social captions (LinkedIn, Facebook, Instagram)
  • Textbook section → 10 carousel bullet points for Canva
  • Email tone pass: "members" language vs. "students"
  • Practice question pull: extract 2–3 sample questions from course content
2

Interactive HTML Resource Docs

Low–Med Effort High Impact Lead Gen

Replace static PDF lead magnets with interactive HTML tools. Higher conversion than a PDF, more memorable, shareable, and brandable. Directly inspired by the interactive demo Yulia loved during the call.

ReplacesStatic PDF downloads as lead magnets
Connects toHubSpot opt-in + welcome sequence
Used byWebsite visitors / prospective members
  • Exam study guide as an interactive HTML page (replaces PDF download)
  • "Is this exam right for me?" quiz — captures lead + routes to correct HubSpot workflow
  • Course preview landing snippet — embeds on site, drives free membership signups
  • Each tool connects to HubSpot form endpoint for list capture
3

HubSpot Email Language Refresh Prompts

Low Effort This Week HubSpot

Yulia's top priority this week is updating "students → members" across her 200+ email library. A targeted Claude prompt handles the language swap + tone review in one pass — turning a week of manual work into a single-day task.

SolvesSubscription → membership migration language backlog
TimelineCan start immediately
ToolsClaude + HubSpot
  • Prompt template: paste any HubSpot email → outputs membership-language version
  • Tone review layer: checks for "student/subscriber" language and flags it
  • Optional: batch processing workflow if volume justifies it
  • Version comparison output so Yulia can approve before replacing
4

SEMrush → Content Brief Template (Structured)

Low Effort Medium Impact SEMrush + Claude

Yulia currently re-explains formatting, length, keywords, and audience every time she writes a ChatGPT prompt from a SEMrush brief. A structured fill-in template eliminates that friction entirely. Same brief → same quality → less editing.

ReplacesRe-writing SEO instructions from scratch each time
ToolsSEMrush + Claude or ChatGPT
Used byYulia
  • Master prompt template with locked fields: audience, tone, exam type, keyword list, heading structure, length, CTA
  • Variable fields Yulia fills in per-article: primary keyword, secondary keywords, source material
  • Output format spec: H1, H2s, word count, meta description, internal link placeholder
  • Variant prompts for: long-form article, short supporting article, email version, social snippet

ToolsMonday.com + Zapier or Make + Claude
Assigns toYulia or India social manager

  • Can connect to Option 5 (repurposing pipeline) to pre-populate card content automatically

Nurture Email Campaign Builder

Medium Effort High Impact HubSpot

Build the bi-weekly educational email Marc described. A repeatable prompt template + structured format that generates a HubSpot-ready email draft from any topic or textbook section — then optionally automates the whole thing via trigger.

InputTopic or textbook section
OutputHubSpot-ready email draft
